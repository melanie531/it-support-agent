1. OVERVIEW
================================================================================

Acme Corporation uses Okta as its primary Identity Provider (IdP) for Single
Sign-On (SSO) across all enterprise applications. All employees, contractors,
and temporary staff are provisioned an Okta account upon onboarding. This
runbook covers password management, self-service reset procedures, MFA
enrollment, and account lockout recovery.

Scope: All Acme Corporation employees, contractors, and vendors with Okta
accounts. This includes access to the following integrated applications:

  - Microsoft 365 (Outlook, Teams, SharePoint, OneDrive)
  - Jira / Confluence (Atlassian Cloud)
  - Salesforce
  - Workday
  - GitHub Enterprise
  - AWS Console (via SAML federation)
  - ServiceNow
  - Slack Enterprise Grid

2. PASSWORD REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

All Okta passwords must meet the following complexity requirements:

  2.1  Minimum length: 12 characters
  2.2  Maximum length: 128 characters
  2.3  Must contain at least ONE character from each of the following categories:
         a) Uppercase letter (A-Z)
         b) Lowercase letter (a-z)
         c) Number (0-9)
         d) Special character (!@#$%^&*()-_=+[]{}|;:',.<>?/)
  2.4  Must NOT contain:
         a) Your first name, last name, or username
         b) Any of your previous 12 passwords
         c) Common dictionary words used alone (e.g., "Password123!")
         d) Sequential characters (e.g., "abcd", "1234")
         e) Repeated characters (e.g., "aaaa", "1111")
  2.5  Password expiration: Every 90 days
  2.6  Password change notification: Users receive email reminders at 14 days,
       7 days, and 1 day before expiration.
  2.7  Recommended: Use a passphrase of 4+ random words with numbers and
       symbols (e.g., "Correct-Horse7!Battery-Staple"). IT recommends using
       the company-provided 1Password account to generate and store passwords.

3. SELF-SERVICE PASSWORD RESET (OKTA)
================================================================================

Prerequisites: You must have previously enrolled in at least one MFA factor
(see Section 4). If you have not enrolled in MFA, contact the IT Help Desk.

  Step 1:  Navigate to https://acmecorp.okta.com in your web browser.
  Step 2:  Click "Need help signing in?" below the login form.
  Step 3:  Click "Forgot password?"
  Step 4:  Enter your Okta username (your corporate email address,
           e.g., jdoe@acmecorp.com).
  Step 5:  Click "Reset via Email" or "Reset via SMS" depending on your
           enrolled recovery factors.
  Step 6:  If you selected email: Check your inbox (and spam/junk folder)
           for the password reset email from noreply@okta.com. The link
           expires in 60 minutes.
  Step 7:  If you selected SMS: Enter the 6-digit code sent to your
           registered mobile number. The code expires in 5 minutes.
  Step 8:  Enter your new password following the requirements in Section 2.
  Step 9:  Confirm the new password by entering it again.
  Step 10: Click "Reset Password."
  Step 11: You will be redirected to the Okta dashboard. Verify you can
           access your applications.

Note: After resetting your password, you may need to re-authenticate on
mobile devices, VPN clients, and cached credentials on your workstation.
Allow up to 15 minutes for the password change to propagate across all
integrated systems.

Troubleshooting:
  - If the reset email does not arrive within 5 minutes, check your spam
    folder and verify you are checking the correct email account.
  - If "Forgot password" is not available, your account may be suspended.
    Contact the IT Help Desk immediately.
  - If you receive "password does not meet requirements," review Section 2
    and ensure all complexity rules are satisfied.

4. MULTI-FACTOR AUTHENTICATION (MFA) SETUP
================================================================================

All Acme Corporation accounts require MFA enrollment. The following factors
are supported, listed in order of preference:

  4.1  Okta Verify (Push Notification) -- RECOMMENDED
       a) Download "Okta Verify" from the Apple App Store or Google Play Store.
       b) Log in to https://acmecorp.okta.com.
       c) Navigate to Settings > Extra Verification.
       d) Click "Set up" next to Okta Verify.
       e) Select your device type (iOS or Android).
       f) Open Okta Verify on your phone and tap "Add Account."
       g) Scan the QR code displayed on your screen.
       h) Tap "Done" once the account appears in Okta Verify.
       i) You will receive a test push notification. Tap "Approve" to
          complete setup.

  4.2  Hardware Security Key (YubiKey)
       a) Request a YubiKey from IT by submitting a ticket at
          https://helpdesk.acmecorp.com (Category: Access Management >
          Hardware Token Request).
       b) Once received, log in to https://acmecorp.okta.com.
       c) Navigate to Settings > Extra Verification.
       d) Click "Set up" next to Security Key or Biometric.
       e) Insert the YubiKey into your USB port.
       f) When prompted, tap the gold contact on the YubiKey.
       g) Name the key (e.g., "Desk YubiKey") and click "Save."
       h) It is strongly recommended to register two keys for redundancy.

  4.3  SMS Verification (Backup Only)
       a) SMS is permitted only as a backup factor. It must not be the
          sole MFA method.
       b) Navigate to Settings > Extra Verification.
       c) Click "Set up" next to SMS Authentication.
       d) Enter your mobile phone number and click "Send code."
       e) Enter the 6-digit verification code and click "Verify."

  4.4  TOTP Authenticator App (Google Authenticator, Authy, 1Password)
       a) Navigate to Settings > Extra Verification.
       b) Click "Set up" next to Google Authenticator.
       c) Open your authenticator app and scan the QR code.
       d) Enter the 6-digit code displayed in your authenticator app.
       e) Click "Verify."

5. MFA RECOVERY PROCEDURES
================================================================================

If you lose access to all enrolled MFA factors (e.g., lost phone, broken
security key), follow these steps:

  Step 1:  Contact the IT Help Desk at helpdesk@acmecorp.com or call
           ext. 5000 (or 555-0100 from an external line).
  Step 2:  Request an MFA reset. You will be asked to verify your identity
           using THREE of the following:
             a) Employee ID number
             b) Last four digits of your SSN (for US employees)
             c) Manager's name and department
             d) Date of birth
             e) Answer to your security question (set during onboarding)
             f) Video call verification with a government-issued photo ID
  Step 3:  Once identity is verified, the Help Desk agent will reset your
           MFA factors in Okta.
  Step 4:  You will receive an email with a temporary access link.
  Step 5:  Log in and immediately re-enroll in at least TWO MFA factors
           (see Section 4).
  Step 6:  Confirm all your applications are accessible.

SLA: MFA reset requests are classified as Priority 2 (High) and will be
processed within 2 hours during business hours (Mon-Fri, 7:00 AM - 7:00 PM
ET). After-hours requests are processed by the on-call team within 4 hours.

6. ACCOUNT LOCKOUT POLICY
================================================================================

Acme Corporation enforces the following account lockout policy to protect
against brute-force attacks:

  6.1  Lockout Threshold: 5 consecutive failed login attempts
  6.2  Lockout Duration: 30 minutes (automatic unlock)
  6.3  Failed Attempt Counter Reset: After 15 minutes of no failed attempts
  6.4  Scope: Lockout applies across all authentication methods (web portal,
       mobile app, API, VPN)

What happens during lockout:
  - All login attempts will be rejected, even with the correct password.
  - You will see the message: "Your account has been locked due to too many
    failed sign-in attempts. Please try again in 30 minutes or contact your
    administrator."
  - Active sessions that were established before lockout will remain active.
  - Automated alerts are sent to the Security Operations Center (SOC) if the
    same account is locked out 3 or more times within 24 hours.

7. UNLOCKING A LOCKED ACCOUNT
================================================================================

Option A: Wait for Automatic Unlock (30 Minutes)
  Step 1:  Wait 30 minutes from the last failed login attempt.
  Step 2:  Try logging in again with the correct password.
  Step 3:  If you are unsure of your password, use the self-service reset
           (Section 3) after the lockout period expires.

Option B: Contact IT Help Desk for Immediate Unlock
  Step 1:  Call the IT Help Desk at ext. 5000 or email helpdesk@acmecorp.com.
  Step 2:  Provide your full name, employee ID, and Okta username.
  Step 3:  The Help Desk agent will verify your identity (name + employee ID
           or name + manager verification).
  Step 4:  The agent will unlock your account in the Okta Admin Console.
  Step 5:  You will be able to log in immediately.
  Step 6:  If you have forgotten your password, the agent can also initiate
           a password reset at this time.

Option C: Manager-Initiated Unlock (When Help Desk Is Unavailable)
  Step 1:  Your direct manager can submit an emergency unlock request via
           https://helpdesk.acmecorp.com (Category: Access Management >
           Emergency Account Unlock).
  Step 2:  The request will be routed to the on-call IAM administrator.
  Step 3:  Expected response time: 1 hour.

8. SERVICE ACCOUNTS AND SHARED MAILBOXES
================================================================================

  8.1  Service accounts (e.g., svc-jenkins@acmecorp.com) follow a separate
       password policy: 24-character minimum, 180-day rotation, no MFA
       (API key or certificate authentication instead).
  8.2  Shared mailbox passwords are managed by the team lead. Password
       changes must be coordinated through the IT Help Desk to update all
       authorized users simultaneously.
  8.3  To request a new service account, submit a Jira ticket under
       IT Service Management > Service Account Request. Approval from your
       manager and the Security team is required (typical turnaround: 3
       business days).

9. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
================================================================================

Q: How do I know when my password is about to expire?
A: You will receive automated email reminders at 14, 7, and 1 day(s) before
   expiration. You can also check in Okta > Settings > Password to see
   your password expiration date.

Q: Can I use the same password as another personal account?
A: Strongly discouraged. Each account should have a unique password. Use
   the company-provided 1Password license to manage your passwords.

Q: What if I am traveling internationally and lose my phone?
A: Contact the Help Desk at +1 (555) 867-5000. International callers can
   also email helpdesk@acmecorp.com. If you have a backup YubiKey, use
   that to authenticate. Otherwise, an MFA reset will be performed per
   Section 5.

Q: Can I use biometric authentication (Face ID, Touch ID)?
A: Yes. Okta Verify supports biometric unlock on enrolled devices. You
   still need at least one additional MFA method configured as backup.

Q: My password reset link expired. What do I do?
A: Request a new one by repeating the steps in Section 3. Reset links are
   valid for 60 minutes.
