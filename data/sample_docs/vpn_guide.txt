1. OVERVIEW
================================================================================

Acme Corporation provides VPN access through Cisco AnyConnect Secure Mobility
Client to enable secure remote connectivity to internal corporate resources.
All remote employees, on-site contractors, and traveling staff must use the
VPN when accessing resources listed in Section 8.

VPN Concentrator: vpn.acmecorp.com
Authentication: Okta SSO with MFA (Okta Verify push or hardware token)
Protocol: TLS 1.3 / DTLS 1.2
Supported Platforms: Windows 10/11, macOS 12+, Ubuntu 20.04+/Fedora 36+

================================================================================
2. VPN ACCESS REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

Before connecting to the VPN, ensure the following:

  2.1  You have an active Acme Corporation Okta account.
  2.2  MFA is enrolled and functioning (see IT-RB-001: Password Reset and
       Account Management).
  2.3  Your device meets endpoint compliance requirements:
         a) Operating system is up to date (within last 2 patch cycles)
         b) Antivirus/endpoint protection is installed and running
            (CrowdStrike Falcon is the corporate standard)
         c) Disk encryption is enabled (BitLocker on Windows, FileVault
            on macOS, LUKS on Linux)
         d) Device is enrolled in the Acme MDM (Jamf for Mac, Intune
            for Windows) -- corporate devices only
  2.4  Personal devices: Must be registered through the BYOD program at
       https://helpdesk.acmecorp.com > BYOD Registration before VPN
       access is provisioned. Allow 1 business day for approval.

3. INSTALLING CISCO ANYCONNECT (WINDOWS)
================================================================================

Option A: Self-Service Install via Software Center

  Step 1:  Open Microsoft Software Center from the Start menu.
  Step 2:  Search for "Cisco AnyConnect."
  Step 3:  Click "Install."
  Step 4:  Wait for installation to complete (approximately 3-5 minutes).
  Step 5:  Restart your computer when prompted.

Option B: Manual Download

  Step 1:  Open a web browser and navigate to https://vpn.acmecorp.com.
  Step 2:  Log in with your Okta credentials.
  Step 3:  Approve the MFA push notification on your phone.
  Step 4:  The AnyConnect installer will download automatically.
           If it does not, click "Download for Windows" on the landing page.
  Step 5:  Run the downloaded installer (anyconnect-win-4.10.xxxxx.exe).
  Step 6:  Accept the license agreement and click "Next."
  Step 7:  Leave the default installation options selected:
             [x] Core & VPN
             [x] Diagnostics and Reporting Tool
             [ ] Posture Module (optional, for compliance checks)
             [ ] Web Security Module (not required)
  Step 8:  Click "Install" and wait for completion.
  Step 9:  Click "Finish."
  Step 10: Restart your computer.

Post-Installation Verification:
  - Open Cisco AnyConnect from the Start menu or system tray.
  - Verify the version is 4.10.06079 or later.
  - Enter "vpn.acmecorp.com" in the connection field and click "Connect"
    to verify it reaches the login portal.

4. INSTALLING CISCO ANYCONNECT (MACOS)
================================================================================

Option A: Self-Service Install via Jamf Self Service

  Step 1:  Open "Self Service" from the Applications folder or Dock.
  Step 2:  Search for "Cisco AnyConnect."
  Step 3:  Click "Install."
  Step 4:  Enter your Mac login password if prompted.
  Step 5:  Allow the system extension when prompted in System Settings >
           Privacy & Security > Security.
  Step 6:  Click "Allow" for the Cisco AnyConnect Socket Filter.
  Step 7:  Restart your Mac.

Option B: Manual Download

  Step 1:  Navigate to https://vpn.acmecorp.com in Safari or Chrome.
  Step 2:  Log in with Okta credentials and approve MFA.
  Step 3:  Download the macOS installer (anyconnect-macos-4.10.xxxxx.dmg).
  Step 4:  Open the DMG file and run "AnyConnect.pkg."
  Step 5:  Follow the installer prompts, accepting the license agreement.
  Step 6:  When the "System Extension Blocked" notification appears:
           a) Open System Settings > Privacy & Security.
           b) Scroll to the Security section.
           c) Click "Allow" next to the Cisco Systems message.
  Step 7:  Grant the required network filter permission when prompted.
  Step 8:  Restart your Mac.

Important macOS Notes:
  - macOS Ventura (13) and later require explicit approval of the system
    extension. The VPN will not function without this approval.
  - If you see "System Extension Update Available," contact IT before
    approving to ensure compatibility.

5. INSTALLING CISCO ANYCONNECT (LINUX)
================================================================================

Supported Distributions: Ubuntu 20.04+, Ubuntu 22.04+, Fedora 36+,
                         RHEL 8+, RHEL 9+

  Step 1:  Download the Linux installer from https://vpn.acmecorp.com
           after authenticating with Okta.
  Step 2:  Extract the archive:
             $ tar -xzf anyconnect-linux64-4.10.xxxxx.tar.gz
  Step 3:  Navigate to the extracted directory:
             $ cd anyconnect-linux64-4.10.xxxxx/vpn/
  Step 4:  Run the installer with root privileges:
             $ sudo ./vpn_install.sh
  Step 5:  Accept the license agreement when prompted.
  Step 6:  Verify installation:
             $ /opt/cisco/anyconnect/bin/vpn --version
  Step 7:  Start the AnyConnect service:
             $ sudo systemctl start vpnagentd
             $ sudo systemctl enable vpnagentd

Dependencies (install if missing):
  Ubuntu/Debian:
    $ sudo apt install libpangox-1.0-0 libpango-1.0-0 libgtk2.0-0
  Fedora/RHEL:
    $ sudo dnf install pangox-compat gtk2

GUI Launch:
  $ /opt/cisco/anyconnect/bin/vpnui

Command-Line Connection:
  $ /opt/cisco/anyconnect/bin/vpn connect vpn.acmecorp.com
  Username: your-okta-username
  Password: your-okta-password
  [Push notification will be sent to Okta Verify]

6. CONNECTING TO THE VPN
================================================================================

  Step 1:  Launch Cisco AnyConnect Secure Mobility Client.
  Step 2:  In the "Connect to" field, enter: vpn.acmecorp.com
  Step 3:  Click "Connect."
  Step 4:  A browser window or embedded login form will appear.
  Step 5:  Select your VPN group from the dropdown:
             - "Acme-Employee" for full-time and part-time employees
             - "Acme-Contractor" for contractors and vendors
             - "Acme-Admin" for IT administrators (restricted access)
  Step 6:  Enter your Okta username (corporate email address).
  Step 7:  Enter your Okta password.
  Step 8:  Approve the MFA push notification on Okta Verify.
           Alternatively, enter your TOTP code or tap your YubiKey.
  Step 9:  Wait for the connection to establish (typically 5-10 seconds).
  Step 10: Verify connection:
           - The AnyConnect icon in the system tray should show a lock icon.
           - The client window should display "Connected to vpn.acmecorp.com."
           - You should be able to ping internal resources:
               $ ping fileserver.internal.acmecorp.com

  To Disconnect:
  Step 1:  Click the AnyConnect icon in the system tray.
  Step 2:  Click "Disconnect."

Session Limits:
  - Maximum session duration: 12 hours (automatic disconnect)
  - Idle timeout: 30 minutes (automatic disconnect if no traffic)
  - Reconnect: You may reconnect immediately after disconnect.

7. SPLIT TUNNELING POLICY
================================================================================

Acme Corporation uses a SPLIT TUNNEL configuration. This means:

  THROUGH THE VPN TUNNEL (corporate traffic):
    - 10.0.0.0/8         (all internal corporate networks)
    - 172.16.0.0/12      (development and staging environments)
    - 192.168.100.0/24   (corporate Wi-Fi at office locations)
    - *.internal.acmecorp.com (internal DNS zone)
    - *.corp.acmecorp.com    (corporate applications)

  DIRECT INTERNET (NOT through VPN):
    - All other internet traffic
    - Public cloud services (AWS, Azure, GCP console)
    - SaaS applications accessed via public URLs
    - Streaming, personal browsing, etc.

Benefits of split tunneling:
  - Faster internet browsing (does not route through corporate datacenter)
  - Reduced VPN bandwidth consumption
  - Better video conferencing quality

Security Note: Even with split tunneling, your corporate endpoint protection
(CrowdStrike Falcon) and DNS filtering (Cisco Umbrella) remain active at
all times. Web traffic is still subject to the Acceptable Use Policy.

10. COMMON VPN ERRORS AND FIXES
================================================================================

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Error: "Connection attempt has failed"
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Cause:   Network connectivity issue or VPN server unreachable.
Fix:
  Step 1: Verify your internet connection by opening a public website.
  Step 2: Try connecting to the backup VPN server: vpn2.acmecorp.com
  Step 3: If on corporate Wi-Fi, try switching to a wired connection.
  Step 4: Flush your DNS cache:
            Windows: ipconfig /flushdns
            macOS:   sudo dscacheutil -flushcache; sudo killall -HUP mDNSResponder
            Linux:   sudo systemd-resolve --flush-caches
  Step 5: Restart the AnyConnect service:
            Windows: Open Services.msc > Restart "Cisco AnyConnect VPN Agent"
            macOS:   sudo launchctl stop com.cisco.anyconnect.vpnagentd
                     sudo launchctl start com.cisco.anyconnect.vpnagentd
            Linux:   sudo systemctl restart vpnagentd
  Step 6: If the issue persists, contact the IT Help Desk.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Error: "VPN agent service is not responding"
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Cause:   The AnyConnect background service has stopped.
Fix:
  Step 1: Restart the vpnagentd service (see commands in previous error).
  Step 2: If the service fails to start, uninstall and reinstall AnyConnect.
  Step 3: On macOS, verify the system extension is approved (System Settings >
          Privacy & Security).

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Error: "Certificate validation failure"
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Cause:   Expired or untrusted certificate on client or server.
Fix:
  Step 1: Ensure your system date and time are correct.
  Step 2: Update the AnyConnect client to the latest version.
  Step 3: Clear the AnyConnect preferences:
            Windows: Delete C:\Users\<you>\AppData\Local\Cisco\
            macOS:   Delete ~/Library/Application Support/Cisco/
  Step 4: Reconnect.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Error: "Login failed" (credentials correct)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Cause:   Account locked, MFA failure, or group policy mismatch.
Fix:
  Step 1: Verify your Okta account is not locked (see IT-RB-001, Section 7).
  Step 2: Ensure you selected the correct VPN group (Employee vs. Contractor).
  Step 3: Try logging in to https://acmecorp.okta.com directly to confirm
          your credentials and MFA work.
  Step 4: If you recently changed your password, try the new password.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Error: "Posture Assessment Failed"
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Cause:   Endpoint does not meet compliance requirements.
Fix:
  Step 1: Ensure your operating system is fully updated.
  Step 2: Verify CrowdStrike Falcon is running (check the system tray icon).
  Step 3: Confirm disk encryption is enabled:
            Windows: Control Panel > BitLocker Drive Encryption
            macOS:   System Settings > Privacy & Security > FileVault
  Step 4: If on a personal device, ensure BYOD enrollment is complete.
  Step 5: Contact IT if the issue persists after verifying all the above.

----------------------------------------------------------------------
Error: "The VPN connection was interrupted - Reconnecting"
----------------------------------------------------------------------
Cause:   Unstable network connection, ISP issues, or Wi-Fi interference.
Fix:
  Step 1: AnyConnect will auto-reconnect. Wait 30 seconds.
  Step 2: If reconnection fails, manually disconnect and reconnect.
  Step 3: Switch to a wired Ethernet connection if possible.
  Step 4: If on Wi-Fi, move closer to the router or switch to 5GHz band.
  Step 5: Disable any conflicting VPN or proxy software.

11. PERFORMANCE OPTIMIZATION
================================================================================

  11.1  Use a wired Ethernet connection when possible for best stability.
  11.2  Close bandwidth-heavy applications not needed during VPN sessions.
  11.3  For large file transfers, use off-peak hours (before 9 AM or
        after 6 PM ET).
  11.4  If video calls are choppy while on VPN, note that Zoom, Teams,
        and Slack calls do NOT go through the VPN (split tunnel) -- if
        these are slow, the issue is your local internet, not the VPN.
  11.5  Regional VPN Gateways:
          - US East:    vpn.acmecorp.com (primary)
          - US West:    vpn-west.acmecorp.com
          - Europe:     vpn-eu.acmecorp.com
          - Asia-Pac:   vpn-apac.acmecorp.com
        Select the gateway closest to your physical location.

12. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
================================================================================

Q: Can I use the VPN on my personal phone or tablet?
A: VPN access is limited to laptops and desktops. Mobile access to corporate
   resources is provided through the Okta Mobile app and Microsoft Intune
   managed apps.

Q: Can I run a third-party VPN alongside Cisco AnyConnect?
A: No. Running two VPN clients simultaneously will cause routing conflicts.
   Disconnect any personal VPN before connecting to the Acme VPN.

Q: Is VPN required when I am in the office?
A: No. When connected to the corporate Wi-Fi (SSID: "AcmeCorp" or
   "AcmeCorp-Secure"), you have direct access to internal resources.

Q: How do I check if I am connected to the VPN?
A: Look for the Cisco AnyConnect icon in your system tray. A padlock icon
   means connected; an open globe means disconnected. You can also run:
     Windows: ipconfig | find "10.255"
     macOS/Linux: ifconfig | grep "10.255"
   A "10.255.x.x" address indicates an active VPN connection.

Q: The VPN is very slow. What can I do?
A: See Section 11. Also try connecting to a different regional gateway.
